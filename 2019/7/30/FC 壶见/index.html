壶见

Commentary

“Hokkien” is equal to “Southern Min” as they refer to the same language, the difference is mainly a geographic one: in Southeast Asia the language is called “Hokkien” and being widely spoken because of the historic immgration from southern Fujian. While in southern Fujian where this language originated and continues staying as its culture center, the name is “Southern Min”. The Chinese brand name “壶见” is a phonetic adaption of “Hokkien” with the selection of Chinese character relevant to drinks (壶). In a Chinese internet meme “Cantonese people eat Hujianese people”, another homophone of this brand name “Hujian(胡建)” is deliberately used to replace the correct version “Fujian(福建)”, better captures the phonemes uttered by Southern Min people themselves (as in “Hokkien”), instead of being pronounced in the Mandarin way.
